Instacart is a real online store which makes some of its data pubicly available. Customer data was synthesised for the purpose of this project. The objective of this project was to answer complex business questions for the marketing and sales teams at Instacart.

In search of answers to questions like:

    What are busiest days of the week?
    What are the busiest hours of the day?
    Are there particular hours of the day where spending is the most?
    What are the price range groupings like?
    Are there certain types pf products are more popular than others?
    Are there differences in ordering habits based on customer loyalty?
    Are there differences in ordering habits based on a customer’s region?
    Is there a connection between age and family status in terms of ordering habits?

To answer these questions and gleam further insights into the sales and operations of Instacart, I used Python and a host of powerful libraries

Tools:

  1. Python
  2. Jupyter Notebooks
  3. Libraries : Pandas, NumPy, Seaborn, Matplotlib,

Data:

  1. >3 Million Rows & 23 variables
  2. open source data
  3. Joins: Order, product, customer & Department

Let's Begin!

Cleaning the Data

The analysis began with consistency checks. Looking for missing values and duplicates in the data was the first step. Also basic descriptive statistics were used to find out if there were any extreme values which may be erroneous.

I also confirmed that were mixed type values in some of the columns which required correction. Jupyter Notebook was used to compile the code into neat and manageable scripts. Use was made of libraries within Python, such as seaborn, pandas, numpy and matplotlib
